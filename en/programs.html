Our Programs

Young Entrepreneur Summit (YES)

YES Girls is an incredible opportunity for young women to collaborate and create a smartphone application that addresses a community need. Students develop apps from scratch before employing business skills to present their final products in front of a panel of judges and compete for awards. The event also features a speaker panel comprised of local entrepreneurs, like Melisa Rillera from Bay Area Tutoring Association and Christine Munroe from NASA.

Innovation Lab

The summer 2016 Innovation Lab is comprised of 3 different courses in business, physical science, and engineering meshed into one week-long summer program.
We teach this program in both Spanish and English, although Spanish is a second language for us (so we'll be learning just as much as you!).

Topics Covered

Intro to Game Development

Sure, playing video games is a blast, but nothing beats making your own! We’ll be teaching students object oriented programming spanning a variety of Python topics like classes, lists, loops, and more, culminating in a final project where we’ll make our own version of the popular computer game, Agar.io.

Model Rocketry

Students will be introduced to basic physics topics including: Newton’s Laws of Motion, aerodynamic forces, and parts of a rocket. They will then put their physics knowledge to the test by designing bottle rockets, which will be launched on the final day of the program.

Reverse Engineering

What’s the best way to learn how something works? Taking it apart! We will be dissecting various electronic devices and exploring the components they contain in this hands-on exploration of hardware engineering. Past device dissections have included: a Samsung Galaxy S4, an MP3 player, and a voice recorder.

Cryptography

The art of cracking codes for security, technology, and secret fun is a centuries-old application of math skills. In our immersive cryptography program, students will learn first about some famous ciphers and their uses before applying them to a scavenger hunt involving solving puzzles to uncover clues in unexpected places!


Student group presentation, August session.

Entrepreneurial Incubator

Anyone can be an entrepreneur. That’s what we hope students will learn in this crash course introduction to the design thinking process, marketing, and presentation skills that will have students collaborating to design a product to solve a problem they face and pitching their solution to the rest of the group.
