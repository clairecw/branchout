The Problem

Over the last few decades, the Silicon Valley has become synonymous with creativity and opportunity, as a hub of technological and scientific innovation. However, access to this opportunity comes at an increasingly high cost to residents, with the San Jose Mercury News reporting that the median price of a home in Santa Clara County just surpassed $1 million, while ethnic diversity, with respect to minorities in local industry, remains dismal. This is especially clear in managerial and engineering positions.

Despite this, the demand for STEM skills is projected to grow to over 9 million jobs between 2012 and 2022 (Bureau of Labor Statistics). Locally, families are expected to (and do) pay upwards of $300 for comparable programs in technology and business.

The Silicon Valley cannot sustain its name as a hub for innovation while socioeconomic barriers continue to restrain opportunity for many families across the region and beyond.
